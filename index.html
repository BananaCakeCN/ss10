July 7 Incident

How did the Pacific side of World War II start?

On July 7th, 1937, a Japanese Unit at Fengtai began its training. It took place at the north of the Wanping City, a place beside to Beijing, currently inside the urban area and boundary of Beijing. When the incident occurred, it is a part of the Chinese territory, not even inside the Japanese occupied zone.

At 10:40 p.m. of July 7th. The Japanese army ended their training due to the sound of gun shots were heard. They went on attendance and quickly found that a private rank (lowest rank) soldier named Shimura Kikujiro (志村菊次郎) was missing. What was actually happening was, this private rank soldier went to poop during the training. When he came back, he found that the other soldiers were gone, so he returned. But on the way back, he got lost, which delayed the time of returned. At 11 p.m., a few Japanese came to the Wanping City and asking permissions from the Chinese army to search for that missing soldier. They refused the request. At 4:20 of the next day, the Japanese army bombed the Wanping City.

The missing soldier returned. But the Japanese commander did not report this to the upper level, and the incident was still going on. The sound of gun shot was by the Japanese soldiers. This was proven after the war by a Japanese commander.

Chiang Kai-shek knew the importance of this incident. If the Japanese Army controls the Wanping City, Chinese will lose access to the Beijing City as they are surrounding it. The Japanese at the same time will also gain the access of railway to the south.

Mao Zedong, the leader of the Communist Party also knew the importance of the incident. They requested to corporate with the Nationalist and joining the Nationalist Army. The red army later became the Eighth route army.

During the 1930s, Japanese armies and other western military forces were settled around Beijing. China was under the civil war. The warlords were fighting against Nanjing Nationalist and other warlords. Most of the people weren’t educated, which made the ideas of Nationalism harder to spread. People were extremely poor, 3-7 million of people per year were dying due to the starving.

Three theories of the start date of World War II

The statement of “World War II started on September 1st, 1939” might not be a fact, but an opinion.

Some historians date the beginning of the World War II to the July 7 Incident on July 7th, 1937. This event marks the start of war on the Pacific.

A few historians, such as Antony Beevor, date the beginning of the World War II to the Manchuria Incident on September 18th, 1931. Which is 8 years earlier than the date of Germany invaded Poland.
